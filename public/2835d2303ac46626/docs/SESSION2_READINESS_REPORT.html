Confirmed Gaps Filled (Cluster Titles)

The following gap clusters were surfaced and addressed in the new drill documents:

  1. Overclaims made before epistemic flags — self-flags arrive too late
  2. Fabricated or unanchored specific numbers (cost figures, benchmark scores, paper IDs, infra stats)
  3. LLaVA-style VLM architecture — conflating text embedding pathway with visual token connector pathway
  4. Applying Transformer-era reasoning to biLSTM-era papers (Lee et al. 2017)
  5. Diffusion Weights — missing LoRA baseline, unamortized efficiency claims, Aghajanyan overreach
  6. Post-training / RLHF — wrong β value, PPO critic memory overclaim, GRPO/DAPO gaps
  7. Domain adaptation — gradient variance framing retracted twice, weight decay asymmetry incomplete
  8. LLM routing — missing MoE/ensembling distinction, compound failure rate, monitoring cost model
  9. Generative models — score matching bridge, flow matching gradient balance overclaim, OT coupling gap
  10. Inference efficiency — wrong KV cache softmax mechanism, MLA RoPE decoupling omitted
  11. MT/multilingual — MLM local discriminativity overclaim, COMET variant lineage, chrF attribution
  12. Pretraining corpus — wrong retrieval-shortcut mechanism, vocabulary size confound
  13. Agentic systems — prompt injection delimiter overclaim, stateful step failure/rollback
  14. Weakest-link papers — synthetic evaluation overclaim, TwinRouterBench baseline, Diffusion Weights undefended
  15. Asserting interviewer's authorship status to the interviewer (highest relational credibility risk)

Drill These Before the Interview — Prioritized Weak Spots

Ranked by severity × frequency of collapse under pressure:

  1. Stop fabricating numbers. Benchmark scores, paper IDs, eBay infra stats (e.g. "480 H100s for a month"), and cost-reduction percentages — if you cannot cite the exact source, say "I don't have the number in front of me." This is the single highest-risk failure mode across all 16 sessions.

  2. Defend your weakest papers. Diffusion Weights and MERA were characterized by Versley and went undefended. Practice one tight 90-second rebuttal for each: what the claim is, what evidence supports it, and what the honest limitation is.

  3. Classical NLP untested territory. EM/Baum-Welch, IBM Models 1–2 (attention as alignment), RST vs PDTB discourse, and CKY vs shift-reduce parsing were never reached in simulation. Do a single timed pass on each — even surface-level recall is better than a blank.

  4. Post-training gaps: DAPO and β. DAPO was entirely absent from every answer. The PPO critic memory "roughly halving" claim and a wrong β value for KL regularization are quick fixes — look them up and lock in the right figures.

  5. MoE vs. routing vs. ensembling distinction. This came up in the routing duel and was not cleanly resolved. Write a three-column comparison (architecture, training signal, inference cost) and rehearse it once out loud.

  6. VLM input pathway separation. The text embedding matrix vs. visual token residual stream injection is a concrete architectural detail Versley will probe. Read the connector section of arXiv:2602.11733 once and be able to draw the data flow.

  7. Self-flag earlier, not later. In 12 of 16 sessions, overclaims arrived before the epistemic hedge. Retrain the habit: state uncertainty in the first sentence, not after the follow-up question lands.

  8. TwinRouterBench accounting defense. The honest self-critique is not "the benchmark favors my method" — it's that the CostSave ranking is metric-sensitive: only the failure-aware variant E (which charges failing trajectories their cost) ranks SR-KNN above ClawRouter; variants A–D rank ClawRouter higher. Defend variant E on first principles — a router that saves money but breaks the task has destroyed value — and be candid that SR-KNN is an in-sample 1-NN upper bound, not a deployable baseline, with a ~10% manual audit backing the execution-verified labels. (Full verified detail in 20_OWN_PAPERS_GROUND_TRUTH.md.)

Residual Risks — Keep an Eye On

  • Synthetic evaluation validity (Human Intent World Model, 91.3% in-distribution). If Versley asks whether this generalizes, the honest answer is "we do not know yet" — but Tony currently defends it as a real-world signal. Prepare a calibrated caveat.
  • Author attribution risk. Tony asserted Versley's authorship status directly to him. This is a relational credibility risk even if factually correct — avoid making claims about your interviewer's CV.
  • Retrofitted thesis framing. The "coherent research narrative" was flagged as post-hoc in the weakest-link session. If asked about thesis coherence, acknowledge the natural evolution rather than over-engineering a unified story.
  • Cost-Adaptive Routing — no experimental results yet. The EMNLP 2026 submission has no published results. Be explicit about this: "We have theoretical framing and preliminary ablations; peer-reviewed results are pending."
  • COMET variant lineage. COMET-22 vs. COMET-DA vs. COMET-QE distinctions and the MQM connection are an easy probe for an MT-aware interviewer. Five minutes with the COMET paper changelog fixes this.
