2.3 Dutch 30% Ruling — 2026 Rules and Net-vs-Gross Impact

What it is: A Dutch tax relief scheme allowing qualifying foreign employees to receive up to 30% of gross salary as a tax-free allowance for "extraterritorial costs." This is not a deduction — 30% of gross is simply not included in the taxable wage.

Tony's eligibility situation:

  • Tony was employed in Madrid (IMDEA Networks MSCA Fellow) until approximately May 2026.
  • Madrid is well over 150 km from the Dutch border (the key geographic requirement).
  • If he joins eBay in H2 2026 and was abroad for at least 16 of the 24 months prior, he likely qualifies.
  • Critical action: Apply within 4 months of the first working day. Confirm with eBay's mobility team or a Dutch tax advisor (CROP, Port Sight Tax) promptly.

2026 thresholds (for the ruling to apply):

Category Minimum Taxable Salary
Standard (any age) €48,013/year
Under 30 with qualifying Master's degree €36,497/year

At €90,000 gross, Tony would qualify under either bracket.

Net-vs-gross impact example at €90,000 gross base:

Scenario Taxable Wage Approx. Annual Tax Approx. Net (rough)
Without 30% ruling €90,000 ~€34,000–€36,000 ~€54,000–€56,000
With 30% ruling €63,000 ~€24,000–€26,000 ~€64,000–€66,000
30% ruling saving — ~€9,000–€10,000/year —

The ruling is effectively worth ~€9,000–€10,000/year in extra take-home pay. Over a 5-year term, that is approximately €45,000–€50,000 in additional after-tax income.

2027 change — important: From January 1, 2027, the rate drops from 30% to 27% for all rulings started on or after January 1, 2024. If Tony starts in H2 2026, he benefits at 30% through December 31, 2026, and at 27% thereafter. The difference on a €90K salary is ~€2,700/year less tax shelter from 2027 onward — not catastrophic but worth knowing. The transitional scheme protecting older ruling holders from this change has expired; it now applies to all new entrants.

Partial non-resident status: Fully phased out from 2025. All 30% ruling holders are taxed as NL residents on global wealth (Box 3).

SECTION 3 — Why eBay: 5–6 Specific Hooks (for the recruiter call and any "why us" moment)

These are Tony's genuine alignment points with eBay's work. Use them conversationally — not as a list to recite.

Hook 1 — LLM Routing Directly Solves Mercury's Cost/Latency Problem

eBay's Mercury platform already orchestrates multiple models (LiLiuM, fine-tuned Llama, Mistral, GCP/Azure external endpoints) for agentic recommendation and shopping experiences. The core challenge Mercury faces — routing queries to the right model at the right cost while meeting latency constraints — is exactly what Tony's three LLM routing papers address: LLM Router (>90% cost reduction vs. single SOTA per step), TwinRouterBench (a step-level agentic LLM-routing benchmark whose trained router cut API cost ~53% vs unrouted Opus at matched resolve on the dynamic track), and MERA (87.3% router accuracy at ~52% of always-large serving cost). This is not superficial alignment; Tony's routing work would be immediately applicable to Mercury's multi-model orchestration stack.

Hook 2 — Domain-Adapted LLMs and the Efficiency Angle (LiLiuM / e-Llama / Vocabulary Paper)

Yannick's team has published a clear arc: LiLiuM (from-scratch pretraining, 34% generation speedup via custom tokenizer) → e-Llama (continued pretraining with ablation-grounded LR and data-ratio choices) → vocabulary customization (20% sequence length reduction, principled monotone efficiency guarantee). Tony's efficiency work resonates at every level: Diffusion Weights achieves 3.2x training speedup by predicting future weight states generatively (evaluated on WMT16 — the same MT benchmark eBay cares about); nnU-Net pruning removes >80% of trained weights while proxy Dice stays >0.95 (no speedup measured — the redundancy is demonstrated, the efficiency win is future work). The shared thread is: deliver the same capability at a fraction of the compute.

Hook 3 — VLM Experience Maps to Magical Listing and Versley's Latest Paper

eBay's Magical Listing is the company's highest-profile GenAI product — fully AI-native, using VLMs + product knowledge graph to cut listing time by >25% and increase new listing creation by >50%. Yannick co-authored the arXiv:2602.11733 (2026) paper on adapting VLMs for e-commerce at scale (SigLIP2 + e-Llama architecture, 4M instruction-tuning examples, multi-image handling). Tony's Human Intent World Model project (VLM fine-tuned on synthetic visual-intent data, inferring customer purchase intent from visual cues) is conceptually the same problem space: teaching a multimodal model to understand visual context in a commercial setting. The direct paper-to-paper overlap gives Tony a concrete technical conversation to have with Yannick.

Hook 4 — Machine Translation: WMT16 Evaluated Work, eBay's Cross-Border Imperative

Cross-border trade is structurally important to eBay (NBER study: 17.5% increase in US exports to Spanish-speaking Latin America from eBay's MT). Tony evaluated Diffusion Weights on WMT16 (translation benchmark), has multilingual NLP experience from the LiLiuM-adjacent space, and understands the RAG-for-MT approach eBay published (BM25 retrieval over bilingual catalog, Mixtral-8x7B, 5-shot = +15% chrF on EN-PL). eBay is actively investing in this area and needs researchers who understand both the LLM-generation side and the MT evaluation rigor.

Hook 5 — Agentic AI: Benchmarking Experience Meets Mercury and Magical Listing

Tony built the Agentic Coding Bench (can an agent plan and build a complete software project from scratch?) — a systematic benchmarking framework for agentic capability at the project level. eBay is in the middle of a platform shift from GenAI features to "scalable agentic experiences" (CEO's Q1 2026 language), with Mercury as the infrastructure and Magical Listing as the flagship product. Tony's benchmarking background is directly useful for evaluating and improving eBay's agentic pipelines, and his agentic harness framework experience translates to Mercury's "prompts as code" architecture.

Hook 6 — Independent Researcher Who Publishes at Scale Without Institutional Infrastructure

eBay's Amsterdam team is an Applied Research team — they publish at ACL, EMNLP, NeurIPS, EACL, ECAI, SIGIR. They need people who can operate at research quality while staying product-grounded. Tony's track record as an independent researcher — producing 5+ works in 6 months, collaborating across Tencent/Gradient Networks/McGill/Tsinghua with no institutional support — is a signal of high output velocity and self-direction. For a team that translates cutting-edge algorithms into production features, this combination of publication discipline and practical systems instinct is exactly the profile.

SECTION 4 — Smart Questions to Ask the Recruiter

These questions serve two purposes: they show genuine interest and research depth, and they surface information you need to evaluate the offer.

On the role and team:

  1. "The job description mentions the Foundation Models team — is this role primarily on the pretraining/continued-pretraining side (which Yannick leads), or does it span post-training and deployment as well?" (Helps understand scope and how closely Tony would work with Yannick day-to-day.)

  2. "What does the publication/research output expectation look like for this role? I see the team publishes regularly at ACL, EMNLP, EACL — is there dedicated research time, or is it primarily applied feature work with research as a secondary output?" (Critical for understanding whether this is a researcher role or a de facto engineering role with a research title.)

  3. "What would a strong first 6 months look like for someone joining this team? What's the balance between onboarding onto existing infrastructure (LiLiuM, e-Llama) versus starting something new?"

On compensation (ask these explicitly and take notes):

  1. "Can you walk me through the full compensation structure? Specifically: is the base quoted inclusive or exclusive of the 8% vakantiegeld? Is there a 13th month bonus in addition to the performance bonus? What is the typical RSU refresh cadence?"

  2. "Will eBay support a 30% ruling application? I moved from Madrid earlier this year and should be within the eligibility window — this is important for my net compensation calculation."

  3. "What is the target bonus percentage for this band, and what has the payout history looked like in recent years (i.e., did eBay hit target and pay out in full)?"

  4. "Is there a training and conference budget for this role? As an active publisher, attending ACL/NeurIPS/EMNLP is professionally important to me."

On process:

  1. "Can you walk me through the full interview process after Stage 1? What are the remaining stages, and what is the typical timeline from Stage 1 to offer?"

  2. "Is there any flexibility on start date? I want to make sure I can close out current commitments cleanly."
