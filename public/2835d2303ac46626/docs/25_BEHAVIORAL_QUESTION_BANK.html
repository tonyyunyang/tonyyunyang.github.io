Behavioral Question Bank — Hiring-Manager Round

A wide net of likely behavioral questions for the 60-minute round with Yannick Versley. This is a routing table, not a script: each row maps a question to a story you've already rehearsed, plus a one-line strategy and the one trap to dodge. Read it the night before, then close it. The polished spoken scripts live in doc 23 (HM Behavioral Playbook); the broader story bank with more options lives in doc 24; the method itself is doc 22 (STAR primer).

How to use this

  • Don't memorize answers — memorize which story to fire. When a question lands, your only job is to recognize the bucket and reach for the right rehearsed story. The words come from docs 22–24; this page is the index that gets you there in two seconds.
  • One story can serve several questions. That's intended. The risk is telling the same anecdote three contradictory ways in one hour — so honor the locks in doc 23 §8 (MeetaVista → P2 only; nnU-Net P1-backup vs P4-primary are different facets; don't run two TwinRouterBench stories back-to-back).
  • Every number in your answer is the verified one (see the cautions below). If a figure isn't in your head, say "I don't have that in front of me — directionally it was…" and give the honest direction. Inventing a number to the man who wrote the neighboring papers is the one fatal move.
  • The discipline that runs through every trap column: caveat goes in the first sentence of your Result; Situation + Task ≤ 45s, whole answer ~2 min, then stop and let him cut in; tell routing/serving stories as "I decided / I did" and hand the floor back — never lecture Versley about routing or his own papers; compensation is a recruiter-only topic — deflect warmly if it surfaces.
  • [CONFIRM TONY] on a row means the mapped story has an interpersonal gap (a real conflict, a named person, a human reaction) that isn't in your record yet. Fill it from a true memory before the room, or fall back to the row's grounded alternative. Never invent the gap.

Verified-number quick card (use these, not the CV)

  • nnU-Net: >80% of weights removed at proxy Dice >0.95, unstructured, no measured speedup, 4 datasets, 5 CV folds, ACDC is the labeled anchor. (Not "99% / 6× / structured.")
  • Eye-tracking: 26 participants, shared-on-request under an agreement, public artifact = the Unity tool; 7 emotions; fusion loses slightly at the 0.5s window (reported, not buried). Not "first large-scale public dataset."
  • TwinRouterBench: 970-step corpus; greedy locking collapses 4^N→~4·N; failure-aware CostSave; variant-E flip = SR-KNN 56.18 vs ClawRouter 53.82; frontier-LLM-as-router flagged "high difficulty" on only 7/147 verified-hard steps; 3rd of 17.
  • MERA: ~87.3% router-decision accuracy at ~52% of always-large serving cost; model-evolution half stuck ~47%, no cumulative gain.
  • Human Intent World Model (MeetaVista): 91.3% is in-distribution only.
  • Reverse Imaging: 4th of 8; RI-T2S underperforms baseline on short-echo-time GRE → motivated RI-Aug.
  • Cost-Adaptive Routing: no results yet. Own it as in-progress, never as a win.
