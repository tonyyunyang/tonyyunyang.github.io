The one rule that beats everything else

Across 16 simulated interviews your #1 risk was inventing numbers under pressure and self-flagging uncertainty too late. If you can't cite a figure exactly, say "I don't have that in front of me" and give the directional claim. Lead with the caveat, not after the follow-up.

Versley's-eye-view — what he'll actually grill you on

He leads LLM pretraining and publishes on doing more with less (a 4B VLM beating a zero-shot 27B; tokenizer customization). So on your own papers he'll zero in on the routing pair — TwinRouterBench + MERA — and Diffusion Weights, and likely skim the three domain papers. The one doc below is PDF-verified: every number is checked, so you answer from fact, not memory.

Three traps to expect: (1) "is Diffusion Weights really diffusion?" — never deny it; it is (real Gaussian forward + DDPM/DDIM reverse + U-Net denoiser), the twist is the future-epoch x₀ target. (2) MERA's 87.3% is router-decision accuracy, not task accuracy. (3) The routing-pair cost accounting — know the failure-aware CostSave and the γ unresolved-instance penalty.
