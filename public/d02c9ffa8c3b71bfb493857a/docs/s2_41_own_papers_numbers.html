Own-Paper Numbers (say them cold)

The single source of truth for your verified figures and their exact framings. Read it last, right before every Stage-2 round — it's the anti-fabrication card so no answer quotes a wrong number. If a figure isn't here, don't say it cold. This doc — "Own-Paper Numbers (say them cold)" — is itself the number source of truth: the per-paper mechanism and framing you need live in the sections below, so nothing to chase elsewhere.

LLM Router

  • What it is: LLM routing for cost-efficiency — send each query to the cheapest model that still clears the quality bar.
  • Say it cold: ">90% cost reduction at matched — iso — quality."
  • Framing: this is the separate LLM Router paper, not TwinRouterBench. State the headline; don't embellish with baselines or mechanism you can't verify.
  • Trap: never let ">90%" and TwinRouterBench's "53%" touch — two different projects.
  • ⚠ [CONFIRM your byline]: the Interview-3 walkthrough leads its headline with this paper's >90%, so an interviewer may ask "and what was your role on that one?" Pin your exact author position before the room. If your contribution here is thinner than on TwinRouterBench/MERA, say it plainly ("on the LLM Router paper I contributed to, the headline was…") rather than presenting it as an owned lead result.

TwinRouterBench

  • What it is: a step-level agentic LLM-routing benchmark — a fast offline static track (970 verified prefixes) plus a live dynamic track on SWE-bench Verified. A benchmark, not a cost-reduction method.
  • Say it cold: "53.1% = 1 − 25.66 / 54.73 — the trained UncommonRoute router spends $25.66 vs $54.73 for unrouted Opus 4.6, at matched resolve (75/100 vs 74/100), on the dynamic track." Also quotable: the same trained router is 6.7× cheaper than the rule-based one ($25.66 vs $172.56).
  • Framing: the 53% is a training-utility sanity check that the static labels transfer — NOT the paper's contribution, NOT a static-benchmark result, and on a 100-case held-out subset. Baseline is Opus 4.6, never GPT-4o.
  • Trap: never say "the benchmark achieves 53%"; never conflate with LLM Router's >90%.

MERA

  • What it is: a trace-driven framework that adapts an agent's per-invocation serving policy (input-only router over strong / cheap / 1.5B specialist + SkillBook + verifier-with-fallback), admitting updates only after offline joint replay.
  • Say it cold: "87.3% router-decision accuracy — how often the learned router's cheap-vs-strong choice matches held-out labels on 832 examples — at 51.8% of always-large serving cost, 4.4% verifier fallback (best four-cycle schedule)."
  • The only task-quality number: the 1.5B GRPO adapter's MBPP eval200 pass rate = ~47.5% vs ~47.0% baseline (flat). The paper reports no end-to-end task success rate anywhere.
  • Trap: 87.3% is ROUTER-DECISION accuracy, never task/answer accuracy. If you slip, correct yourself in the same breath.

Diffusion Weights

  • What it is: reframes fine-tuning as generative forecasting — a diffusion U-Net predicts a future ("next-epoch") weight state in a β-VAE latent space instead of running SGD.
  • Say it cold: "~3.2× wall-clock fine-tune speedup at comparable accuracy, GPT-2 up to 1.5B on a single 4090." Note the gap: 3.2× is the conservative abstract headline; Table 1 per-task is 3.8–4.3× — pre-empt it, don't reconcile with invented math.
  • Framing: it IS genuine diffusion — real Gaussian forward noising, DDPM/DDIM reverse chain, attention U-Net denoiser. Diffusion-inspired only because of the latent space, the future-state (x₀) target, and ControlNet conditioning. Anonymized ACL submission under review — "a paper I'm an author on," not "accepted."
  • Trap: do NOT disown it as "not really diffusion / just MSE regression" — flatly contradicted by the PDF.

Pruning nnU-Net

  • What it is: one-shot post-training magnitude pruning on a trained nnU-Net; the real finding is an inverted-U redundancy pattern — the bottleneck and deep layers hold the most parameters yet are the most prunable.
  • Say it cold: ">80% of weights removed while proxy Dice stays >0.95 across four 2D/3D medical datasets." (Proxy Dice = agreement between pruned and unpruned predictions; ACDC is the one dataset with a true-Dice curve, essentially flat through ~80%.)
  • Framing: FIRST author. There is no speedup / latency / FLOPs / memory number in the paper — efficiency is framed as a future "opportunity" (unstructured pruning leaves zeros you still store and multiply).
  • Trap: NEVER say "99% sparsity" or "6× speedup" — both fabricated. 99.1% is only Brain Tumor at the most aggressive threshold (a high-degradation point), not the minimal-loss claim.

Reverse Imaging (cardiac MRI)

  • What it is: recovers sequence-invariant spin properties (PD, T1, T2) via an MR-physics inverse problem regularized by a diffusion "spin prior," then re-renders any sequence through its Bloch forward model — zero-shot cross-sequence segmentation with no target data.
  • Say it cold: "RI-Aug takes MOLLI LV Dice 39.9 → 91.6 and Device MYO 86.9 → 93.1, no target-domain data; RI-Aug best on both eval sets, all significant at p<0.05." Baselines: nnU-Net and BayeSeg.
  • Framing: 4th of 8 (lead Yidong Zhao, corresponding Qian Tao). arXiv preprint (Aug 2025) in MICCAI/LNCS style. The gap is cross-sequence (bSSFP vs MOLLI vs GRE), not cross-vendor/field-strength.
  • Trap: never say MICCAI 2025 or IEEE-TMI as fact; never describe it as image feature-matching / style transfer. Spin maps are "approximate but meaningful," never quantitatively validated.

Eye-Tracking VR emotion (IMWUT)

  • What it is: the first VR emotion dataset to capture raw high-frame-rate periocular video plus 240 Hz gaze with discrete Ekman labels; the benchmark model is a multi-faceted fusion (ViViT on periocular video + time-series transformer on eye-movement).
  • Say it cold: "Fusion lifts cross-session F1 0.54 → 0.70 (periocular-only → fused); five-shot personalization reaches 0.70 cross-session, 0.62 cross-study. 26 participants, 240 Hz gaze (4× VREED's 60 Hz), Ekman's 7 emotions."
  • Framing: FIRST author; Bishwas Regmi is equal-contribution co-first. Headline modality is the periocular video — gaze/eye-movement alone is the weakest path (~0.23 cross-session). Dataset is available on request after a sharing agreement; the public artifact is the Unity tool.
  • Trap: never pitch as "gaze-only" or "a transformer on gaze gets 0.71." Co-first is Bishwas, not "Bharat." Own the honest 0.5s-window dip (fusion 0.43 vs periocular 0.45). No EEG anywhere.
