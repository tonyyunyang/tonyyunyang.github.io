Behavioral & Competency Bank

Your routing table for Interview 3's STAR half — a fast index that maps each likely question to the story you fire, the one-line strategy, and the trap to dodge. Read it the night before, then close it. The full spoken tellings live in STAR Stories — The Four Prompts; the project narrative lives in The Project Walkthrough; the ops-gap answer lives in The Honest MLOps-Gap Script.

How to use this

  • Don't memorize answers — memorize which story to fire. When a question lands, your only job is to recognize the bucket and reach for the right rehearsed story. The words come from the STAR docs; this page gets you there in two seconds.
  • One story serves several questions. That's intended. The risk is telling the same anecdote three contradictory ways in one hour — keep each story's facts fixed and just re-angle the emphasis.
  • Every number is the verified one (see the quick card at the bottom). If a figure isn't in your head, say "I don't have that in front of me — directionally it was…" and give the honest direction. Inventing a number is the one fatal move — especially on your own papers.
  • Discipline that runs through every Trap column: caveat goes in the first sentence of your Result; Situation + Task ≤ 45s, whole answer ~2 min, then stop and let them cut in; say "I" not "we" in the Action; tell routing stories as "I decided / I did," then hand the floor back.
  • ⚠ [CONFIRM TONY] on a row means the mapped story has an interpersonal gap — a real conflict, a named person, a human reaction — not yet in your record. Fill it from a true memory before the room, or fall back to the row's grounded alternative. Never invent the gap.

1. The core prompts (rehearse these cold)

These four are the ones you were asked to prepare in writing — expect at least one verbatim.

Burning the clock on Situation; claiming production deployment / live traffic / dollar savings that didn't happen.
A challenging project and how you overcame the difficulty. 2. Program & project management, delivery

How did you plan and sequence the work? ROUTE Benchmark → simple scalar router → skill-adaptive router; each phase gated on a measured result before adding complexity. A vague "we iterated"; no gating logic.
How did you know you were on track? ROUTE Pre-set success metrics (quality margin, cost %, routing overhead); per-milestone eval on TwinRouterBench. Retrofitting metrics you set after the fact.

Something you built that no one asked for. TRB You built the measuring stick first — a single router's number is unfalsifiable; the benchmark made every later claim comparative. Framing it as scope-creep rather than deliberate judgement.

Closing reminders (the five that lose offers)

  1. Never invent a number. "I don't have that in front of me — directionally it was…" Verified figures only. Your own papers are the highest-risk place to overclaim.
  2. Caveat first, in the opening sentence of your Result — not after a follow-up drags it out.
  3. S + T ≤ 45s; whole answer ~2 min; then stop. Let them cut in and steer.
  4. Say "I," not "we," in the Action. Use "we" only for genuine team context, then switch to "I did X" immediately.
  5. Own the ops gap once, cleanly (The Honest MLOps-Gap Script), and don't relitigate it. Honesty + concept-fluency + pivot beats a polished-sounding fabrication every time.
