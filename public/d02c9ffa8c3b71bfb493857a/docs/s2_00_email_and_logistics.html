When Ellen (Candidate Experience Partner) reaches out

She coordinates availability. A few things worth confirming with her (low-key, in the scheduling thread):

  • Spacing: Ask if the three 60-min interviews are on one day or spread across days. If you can, avoid all three back-to-back in one block — the coding round in particular deserves a fresh head. Propose a sensible order if asked.
  • Project walkthrough format: Ask whether the Project Management & Retrospective round expects slides or a verbal walkthrough (and screen-share vs. talk-through). This changes how you prepare that session. (If slides: keep it to ~6-8 light slides — architecture, key decisions, results, retrospective.)
  • Tooling for live coding: Ask what environment the live coding uses (shared editor / CoderPad / their own tool) and confirm Python is fine (it is — you'll use Python).
  • Interviewers: If offered, get the names/roles of the three interviewers so you can tailor rapport. (Expect three different people, likely not Versley.)
  • Time zone / your availability: You're Amsterdam-based, so scheduling is easy. Offer a few concrete slots to move fast.

Your locked logistics (only if asked — don't volunteer)

  • Comp: Leave to the recruiter. Don't raise it with interviewers.
